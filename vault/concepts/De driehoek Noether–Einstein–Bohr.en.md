# The Noether–Einstein–Bohr triangle

## Kern
Between 1905 and 1927, three revolutions occurred simultaneously in physics: Einstein's relativity, Bohr's quantum mechanics, and Noether's symmetry framework. They are rarely discussed together, yet Noether's work is the mathematical glue that connects the other two — and explains why they clash.

## Uitleg
The three storylines run parallel in time but are seldom told as one whole.

**Einstein (1905–1915)** discovers that space and time are not absolute but elastic — they deform with velocity (special relativity, 1905) and with mass (general relativity, 1915). But his theory creates a problem: in a curved, dynamic spacetime, it is no longer obvious what "conservation of energy" means. If space itself changes, where does the energy go? Einstein presents this problem to the mathematicians at Göttingen.

**Noether (1915–1918)** takes on that problem and not only solves it but discovers something far deeper. Her theorem proves that energy conservation is a consequence of time symmetry — the assumption that the laws of physics work the same tomorrow as today. In general relativity, that symmetry is subtly broken: the universe expands, the "rules" shift on cosmic scales, and therefore energy on the scale of the entire universe is not strictly conserved. Einstein's intuition was correct: there was indeed something strange about energy. Noether laid bare precisely what.

**Bohr (1913–1927)** meanwhile revolutionises the physics of the very small. His atomic model (1913) introduces discrete energy levels — electrons can only occupy certain orbits, nothing in between. Heisenberg formulates the uncertainty principle in 1927: you cannot simultaneously know the exact position and momentum of a particle. The same applies to energy and time.

Here the connection with Noether becomes deep. Those "pairs" that Heisenberg discovers — position/momentum, energy/time — are not arbitrary. Noether's theorem explains precisely why *these* quantities are coupled:

- Position and momentum are coupled because momentum arises from spatial symmetry (the laws are the same at every location). Position is the variable associated with that symmetry.
- Energy and time are coupled because energy arises from time symmetry (the laws are the same at every moment). Time is the variable associated with that symmetry.

The uncertainty principle is therefore not an accidental limitation of our measuring equipment. It is a mathematically inevitable consequence of the symmetry structure of nature — the structure Noether uncovered.

**What this means taken together:** Einstein showed that space and time are dynamic. Bohr showed that energy and matter are discrete. Noether showed that the laws governing both worlds are rooted in symmetry — and that when that symmetry breaks, the familiar conservation laws go with it.

The great unsolved question of modern physics — how do you combine Einstein's gravity (continuous, smooth spacetime) with Bohr's quantum mechanics (discrete, probabilistic matter)? — is essentially the question of which symmetries survive at the deepest level. Noether's framework is the language in which that answer will be formulated, if it is ever found.

## Waarom het ertoe doet
The three revolutions are often taught separately: relativity in one course, quantum mechanics in another, and Noether is skipped. But without Noether, the story lacks its thread. She is the one who shows that conservation laws, uncertainty relations and the structure of spacetime are not three separate facts but three expressions of the same principle: symmetry.

It is also a story about who we remember. Einstein is an icon. Bohr is known to anyone who touches physics. Noether — whose work is at least as fundamental — you have to look up. That she was a woman at a time that did not admit women to the university explains the obscurity but does not justify it.

## Open vragen
- If quantum gravity is ever found, what symmetry underlies it? And what conservation law comes with it?
- Carroll argues that the expansion of the universe breaks time symmetry and thereby invalidates energy conservation on cosmic scales. How far do the consequences of that reach?
- Noether's theorem applies to *continuous* symmetries. Quantum mechanics also features *discrete* symmetries (such as matter/antimatter). How do those relate to Noether?
