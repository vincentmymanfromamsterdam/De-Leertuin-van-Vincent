# The hedgehog as garden resident

## Core
The hedgehog is the only wild mammal that most Dutch people regularly encounter in their garden, and at the same time one of the fastest declining. The way you garden determines whether it can survive.

## Explanation
The European hedgehog (*Erinaceus europaeus*) is a nocturnal animal that feeds on insects, slugs, earthworms, beetles, and occasionally a frog or mouse. An adult hedgehog eats the equivalent of a third of its body weight per night — making it one of the most effective natural pest controllers in any garden.

But the hedgehog population in the Netherlands has declined sharply in recent decades. The main causes are not the usual suspects (pesticides, climate) but something more mundane: habitat fragmentation. Hedgehogs have a home range of 2–5 hectares and travel up to 2 kilometres per night. They need to be able to move between gardens, parks, and green corridors. Every impenetrable fence, every closed garden boundary, is a wall in their world.

The simplest and highest-impact measure a garden owner can take is a "hedgehog gate": a 13×13 cm hole at the bottom of the fence. Large enough for a hedgehog but too small for most cats. If neighbours do the same, a network of connected gardens emerges — a hedgehog highway through the neighbourhood.

Hedgehogs also depend on a degree of mess. A tidy garden with short lawn, no leaf piles, and no shrubs offers no shelter, no overwintering site, and little food. What a hedgehog needs: a leaf pile or brush heap to hide and hibernate in, a compost heap (full of worms and beetles), no robotic mower at night (lethal), and no slug pellets (poisoning through the food chain — the hedgehog eats the poisoned slug).

The hedgehog is what is called an "umbrella species": if you make your garden suitable for hedgehogs, you automatically make it suitable for a whole range of other species — toads, solitary bees, [[Earthworms as soil builders|earthworms]], soil insects. The hedgehog is the indicator that the whole is working.

## Why this matters
The hedgehog is the most visible and recognisable representative of garden biodiversity. A garden in which a hedgehog lives is a garden in which the [[Soil food web]] functions, where insects provide enough food, and where the structure has enough variation. It is also the species easiest to help: a single hole in the fence can make the difference.
