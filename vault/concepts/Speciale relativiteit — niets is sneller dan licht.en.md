# Special relativity — nothing is faster than light

## Core
In 1905 Einstein showed that the speed of light is the same for everyone, regardless of how fast you are moving. That simple fact forces astonishing conclusions: time slows at high speed, lengths contract, and mass and energy are interchangeable (E=mc²).

## Explanation
It begins with a paradox. Imagine you are on a train travelling at 90% the speed of light, and you shine a flashlight forward. Classically you would expect the light from the flashlight to move at the speed of light plus the speed of the train — but that doesn't happen. The light moves at exactly the speed of light, regardless of the train's speed. That is not an approximation but an experimental fact.

Einstein accepted this as an axiom and worked out the consequences. If the speed of light is constant, then space and time must bend to make that possible.

Time dilation: a clock that is moving ticks more slowly than a stationary clock. Not because something is wrong with the clock, but because time itself runs more slowly at higher speeds. This has been experimentally confirmed with atomic clocks in aircraft and with unstable particles in accelerators: a muon travelling at nearly the speed of light lives measurably longer than a stationary muon.

Length contraction: an object that is moving shrinks in the direction of motion. A rocket flying past you at 90% the speed of light is measurably shorter than when it is at rest.

And the most famous consequence: E=mc². Mass and energy are two forms of the same thing. A small amount of mass contains an enormous amount of energy — that is what powers nuclear weapons and stars (via [[Stellar nucleosynthesis]]).

Sagan explains in *Cosmos* why we don't notice this in daily life: the effects only become significant at speeds close to the speed of light. At 100 km/h time dilation is negligibly small. But GPS satellites move fast enough that without relativistic corrections, navigation would drift by metres per day.

Feynman emphasises that special relativity is not "a theory" in the everyday sense of the word. It is one of the best-tested results in all of physics. Every particle accelerator in the world is a daily confirmation.

## Why this matters
It fundamentally changes what space and time are. They are not the unchanging stage on which physics plays out (Newton's picture), but elastic quantities that deform along with motion. That is the beginning of a path leading to general relativity, [[Black holes]], and the structure of the universe itself.

## Open questions
- If nothing can travel faster than light, how can galaxies beyond our observable universe recede from us "faster than light"? (Hint: it is space that expands, not the galaxies that move)
- Why is the speed of light precisely what it is? Is it a fundamental constant or does it follow from something deeper?
